Chickens are one of the more useful passive mobs in the game, due to their low health, decent spawn rates and good drops.
Chickens drop 0-2 feathers and 1 raw chicken when they’re killed.
Chickens will also drop experience orbs, granting the player 1-3 experience points.
Like other meat dropping mobs, chicken's meat is cooked if they die by fire.
Chickens will drop eggs randomly every 5 to 10 minutes.
Chickens will spawn in the overworld in groups of 2-6, but it’s quite common to find several of these groups close to each other.
They can only spawn on grass blocks in a light level of 9 or higher.
Chickens can spawn when you shoot an egg on the ground.
There’s a 1/8 chance of a baby chicken hatching from an egg.
It’s possible for 4 baby chickens to spawn from 1 egg, though there’s only a 1/256 change of this happening.
This makes chickens the overworld-only mob which can appear in the nether and the end, without the use of mods or monster eggs.
Chickens are one of the only mobs that can’t die from fall damage.
Rather than dropping like a brick, chickens will flap their little wings and slowly glide down, before gently landing on their feet.
Chickens can ride a minecart.
A chicken will not slow down a falling minecart.
Chickens can be bred using seeds.
Rather than laying an egg after they’ve mated, chickens will give birth to live offspring.
Ocelots will kill any chicken they can get their claws on.
Cats will leave your chickens alone.
Chickens and their eggs can be farmed in various ways, ranging from a simple field to an automated system.
